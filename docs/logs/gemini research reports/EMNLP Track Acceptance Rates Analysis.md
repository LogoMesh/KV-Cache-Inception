# **Empirical Analysis of Acceptance Rates and ARR Cycle Statistics for EMNLP (2024–2025)**

The following report analyzes the acceptance rates and ACL Rolling Review (ARR) cycle statistics for the Conference on Empirical Methods in Natural Language Processing (EMNLP) across the 2024 and 2025 cycles. The analysis specifically targets three subject-area tracks: "Efficient Methods for NLP," "Interpretability and Analysis of Models for NLP," and "Machine Learning for NLP."

The data presented relies on official EMNLP chair reports, ARR statistics dashboards, published proceedings, and community-sourced aggregations. Because the EMNLP review process relies on a multi-stage pipeline fed by decentralized ARR cycles, acceptance calculations consider papers committed to the venue after receiving ARR meta-reviews.1

## **1\. Cycle: EMNLP 2024 (Miami)**

The 2024 EMNLP conference was held in Miami, Florida, from November 12 to November 16, 2024\.3 All submissions were processed via the ARR system, requiring authors to commit fully reviewed papers to the conference by August 20, 2024\.4

### **Target-Track Acceptance Rates**

Official EMNLP 2024 track-level acceptance rates, including specific submission denominators and accepted paper counts for individual tracks, are **not published** in the venue proceedings, chair reports, or ARR statistical dashboards.2

* **Track \#6 "Efficient Methods for NLP"**: Not published.6  
* **Track \#13 "Interpretability and Analysis of Models for NLP"**: Not published.6  
* **Track \#17 "Machine Learning for NLP"**: Not published.6

### **Aggregate EMNLP 2024 Acceptance Rates**

Due to the absence of track-specific data, the aggregate acceptance statistics for the entire EMNLP 2024 cycle are provided. The source for this data is the official EMNLP 2024 Main Conference and Findings proceedings.2

The calculation of the aggregate acceptance rate for EMNLP 2024 utilizes a total denominator of 6,105 valid submissions.2 This denominator was derived from an initial pool of 6,395 submissions, from which 70 papers were withdrawn prior to decision and 220 were desk-rejected.2

| EMNLP 2024 Acceptance Category | Accepted Papers | Acceptance Rate |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **Main Conference** | 1,271 | 20.80% |
| **Findings of EMNLP** | 1,029 | 16.86% |
| **Overall (Main \+ Findings)** | 2,300 | 37.66% |

The 1,271 Main Conference papers comprised 1,125 long papers and 143 short papers (with 3 papers withdrawn post-acceptance).2 The 1,029 Findings papers—defined officially as submissions not accepted to the Main Conference but judged worthy of publication as "solid work with sufficient substance, quality and novelty"—comprised 874 long papers and 129 short papers (with 26 withdrawn post-acceptance).7

While rates for the three target tracks remain unpublished, EMNLP 2024 did publish data for alternative tracks. The Industry Track received 334 submissions and accepted 121 papers (36.00% acceptance rate).8 The Special Theme Track ("Efficiency in Model Algorithms, Training, and Inference") received 138 submissions, accepting 54 to the Main Conference and 42 to Findings, yielding a highly lenient overall track acceptance rate of 69.56%.2

## **2\. Cycle: ARR Feeding EMNLP 2024 (Oct 2023 – April 2024\)**

The EMNLP 2024 commitment pool was primarily fed by ARR cycles running from late 2023 through mid-2024. Specifically, the 6,105 papers in the EMNLP 2024 denominator included 526 papers that explicitly selected EMNLP during the ARR April/June 2024 cycle, 5,797 papers from that same cycle that did not specify a venue, 7 papers that initially targeted another conference, and 65 papers originating from ARR cycles prior to April 2024\.7

### **Track Quality Observations**

No verbatim quotes regarding the qualitative competitiveness of the three target tracks were published during the EMNLP 2024 opening or closing remarks. However, the chair report for the 18th Conference of the European Chapter of the Association for Computational Linguistics (EACL 2024), which accepted commitments directly from the October 2023 ARR cycle, explicitly noted the popularity of one of the target tracks. The chairs stated:

"The three areas to receive the largest number of submissions were NLP Applications, Resources and Evaluation, and Interpretability and Analysis of Models for NLP." 9

## **3\. Cycle: EMNLP 2025 (Suzhou)**

The 2025 EMNLP conference was held in Suzhou, China, from November 4 to November 9, 2025\.11 The conference maintained the two-stage ARR submission and commitment process, establishing an August 1, 2025, commitment deadline.1

### **Target-Track Acceptance Rates**

Consistent with the previous year, official EMNLP 2025 track-level acceptance rates are **not published** in the official proceedings or chair reports.6

* **Track \#6 "Efficient Methods for NLP"**: Not published.6  
* **Track \#13 "Interpretability and Analysis of Models for NLP"**: Not published.6  
* **Track \#17 "Machine Learning for NLP"**: Not published.6

While official data is absent, the PaperCopilot community tracking platform collected opt-in self-reported data for EMNLP 2025\. Across 59 valid initial community responses, the average overall reviewer score for EMNLP 2025 submissions was 3.14 (standard deviation: 0.32), with scores ranging from a minimum of 2.30 to a maximum of 3.60.13 Within this limited dataset, self-reported papers in the "Interpretability, Model Editing, Transparency, and Explainability" track showed average scores between 3.17 and 3.67, while papers in "Mathematical, Symbolic, and Logical Reasoning in NLP" (organized as a sub-area under the ARR "Machine Learning for NLP" track for 2025 14) scored around 3.50.13

### **Aggregate EMNLP 2025 Acceptance Rates**

The aggregate acceptance rates for the EMNLP 2025 cycle are documented in the official Message from the Program Chairs within the EMNLP 2025 Proceedings.12

The acceptance rate calculation for EMNLP 2025 utilizes an expanded total denominator of 8,174 papers.12 This calculation explicitly incorporates the multi-stage ARR process, counting all papers committed to EMNLP from the December 2024 ARR cycle through the May 2025 ARR cycle.12 This denominator retains 778 papers that were withdrawn before receiving three reviews and 670 desk-rejected papers.12

| EMNLP 2025 Acceptance Category | Accepted Papers | Acceptance Rate |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **Main Conference** | 1,811 | 22.16% |
| **Findings of EMNLP** | 1,417 | 17.34% |
| **Overall (Main \+ Findings)** | 3,228 | 39.50% |

The Main Conference proceedings consisted of 1,668 long papers and 143 short papers.12 The Findings proceedings included 1,246 long papers and 149 short papers.12

## **4\. Cycle: ARR Feeding EMNLP 2025 (Oct 2024 – April 2025\)**

The 8,174 papers comprising the EMNLP 2025 denominator were sourced from the following ARR cycles: 7,916 papers from May 2025; 220 papers from February 2025; 25 papers from December 2024; 11 papers from October 2024; 1 paper from August 2024; and 1 paper from June 2024\.12

### **Target-Track ARR Score Distributions**

The ARR per-cycle reports for the October 2024 through April 2025 iterations do not break down score distributions by track.6

* **Track \#6 "Efficient Methods for NLP"**: Score distributions not published.6  
* **Track \#13 "Interpretability and Analysis of Models for NLP"**: Score distributions not published.6  
* **Track \#17 "Machine Learning for NLP"**: Score distributions not published.6

To improve the granularity of feedback, the February 2025 ARR cycle introduced a new scoring rubric requiring reviewers to provide three distinct scores: "soundness," "excitement," and an "overall recommendation".17 The guidelines define the overall recommendation as a composite metric reflecting soundness, excitement, novelty, and impact.18 "Soundness" evaluates technical validity and experimental rigor, while "excitement" is a subjective measure of whether a paper presents a conceptual breakthrough, challenges assumptions, or enables new applications.20 Despite the collection of these metrics, track-level distributions of these specific dimensions are not published in the ARR reports.6

### **Track Quality Observations**

While the EMNLP 2025 chair reports do not contain verbatim quotes singling out specific tracks for their competitiveness, the chairs did comment extensively on the operational quality of the peer-review pipeline across all tracks. Specifically, the chairs reported that "the ARR process ran somewhat less smoothly than ideal, with more than 50% of submitted papers missing at least one review by the deadline".12 Consequently, the chairs enforced a new accountability policy, deeming 69 reviewers "highly irresponsible" for failing to submit 100% of their reviews without filing an emergency declaration, resulting in desk rejections for the work of those individuals.12

## **5\. Visible Trends**

Based on the available aggregate data and conference reporting, the following visible trends can be observed regarding the submission pipeline:

1. **Strictness and Leniency Cannot Be Isolated by Track:** The prompt asks if one of the three target tracks is historically stricter or more lenient. Because both ARR and EMNLP decline to publish track-level denominators and accepted paper counts, there is no official empirical data to prove that "Efficient Methods for NLP," "Interpretability and Analysis of Models for NLP," or "Machine Learning for NLP" experiences higher or lower rejection rates than the venue average.6  
2. **Stable, Slightly Lenient Cycle-to-Cycle Aggregate Trend:** Despite explosive growth in submission volumes—evidenced by the total EMNLP denominator scaling from 6,105 in 2024 2 to 8,174 in 2025 12—the conference has maintained cycle-to-cycle stability with a slight trend toward leniency. The Main Conference acceptance rate rose from 20.80% in 2024 to 22.16% in 2025, and the Findings acceptance rate rose from 16.86% to 17.34%.2 The overall likelihood of acceptance (Main \+ Findings) increased cycle-to-cycle from 37.66% to 39.50%.2  
3. **High Relative Competitiveness of Interpretability:** While absolute acceptance rates are unavailable, "Interpretability and Analysis of Models for NLP" was explicitly identified as one of the three largest areas by submission volume during the early 2024 ARR cycles.9 This high submission density inherently creates a highly competitive environment for securing oral presentations or Main Conference designations within that specific track.
