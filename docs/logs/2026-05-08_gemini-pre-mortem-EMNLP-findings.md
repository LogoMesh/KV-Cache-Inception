The recent structural recast—designated internally as Option \\alpha+—represents a deliberate pivot away from a phenomenon-centric headline (alignment faking detection) toward an infrastructure-centric headline (the Reversible MCTS algorithm). The following analysis provides an exhaustive pre-mortem dissection of the manuscript as it stands in the May 8, 2026 compilation. ### **Failure Mode 1: The Scale-Capability Epistemological Confound**

The most profound methodological flaw in the current experimental design is the severe mismatch between the theoretical phenomenon being investigated and the empirical apparatus deployed to measure it. The manuscript is motivated by the threat of "alignment faking" and the "orthogonal escape" of deceptive representations, citing Anthropic's landmark study on Claude 3 Opus and Hubinger et al.'s work on sleeper agents. These are highly advanced, capability-dependent behaviors documented exclusively in frontier-scale models (typically exceeding 70 billion parameters).  
Experiment 1 executes an \\alpha-sweep on Llama 3.2-1B-Instruct and independently confirms reversibility on Llama 3.2-3B-Instruct. The results indicate that the Telemetry Divergence Score (TDS) decreases monotonically with intervention magnitude, which directly contradicts the signature predicted by the Orthogonal Escape Hypothesis. The manuscript characterizes this honestly as an "anti-OE empirical observation" and aligns it with the negative-result regime reported for input-space attacks by Bailey et al. (2024).  
An EMNLP reviewer will immediately identify this as an epistemological trap. A negative result is only scientifically meaningful if a positive result was theoretically reachable within the confines of the experiment. Because the 1B-parameter model lacks the vast, high-dimensional representational capacity required to simultaneously simulate compliance and encode complex deceptive intent in orthogonal null-spaces, the failure to observe orthogonal escape is a trivial inevitability. The reviewer cannot ascertain whether the Reversible MCTS algorithm effectively suppresses orthogonal escape, or if it is merely operating on a model fundamentally incapable of the behavior. The manuscript attempts to mitigate this with a disclaimer in the Interpretation paragraph, stating that the results characterize the measurement framework rather than the phenomenon at capability-dependent scales. However, EMNLP reviewers actively penalize disclaimers that attempt to excuse core methodological deficiencies; if the model cannot exhibit the phenomenon, it is the wrong testbed for the metric.  
**Successful EMNLP Precedent:** The EMNLP 2025 accepted paper *SHARP: Steering Hallucination in LVLMs via Representation Engineering* (Wu et al., 2025\) navigated this concern flawlessly. Rather than testing a highly advanced phenomenon on a small model, the authors focused on *hallucination*—a behavior intrinsically present across all model scales—and validated their representation engineering interventions across a suite of models ranging from 7B to much larger architectures.  
**Corrective Action:** The interpretation of Experiment 1 must be aggressively pivoted. The manuscript must explicitly abandon the pretense that the 1B/3B evaluation is a test of the Orthogonal Escape Hypothesis. The narrative must reframe Experiment 1 purely as an architectural validation of the Reversible MCTS telemetry pipeline's responsiveness and stability in high-dimensional saturation regimes. Alternatively, the experimental target must be swapped to a phenomenon that Llama 3.2-1B genuinely exhibits—such as sycophancy or contextual hallucination—allowing the \\sigma\_H (H-Neuron) signal to be evaluated against a phenomenon that actually exists in the experimental model's latent space.

### **Failure Mode 2: Venue-Mismatched Algorithmic Foregrounding**

The Option \\alpha+ recast strategy sought safety by retreating to the mathematically unassailable elements of the research: the Reversible MCTS algorithm, the FP32 accumulator mechanism, Theorem 1 (Exact Reversibility), and the Memory Complexity Proposition. Consequently, the Abstract and Section 1.1 heavily foreground the elimination of the O(b^d \\cdot M\_{\\text{KV}}) parallel-cache overhead in favor of a constant-factor memory bound.  
This framing guarantees friction at EMNLP. The EMNLP reviewing pool consists of linguists, empirical scientists, and interpretability researchers who prioritize advancements in natural language understanding, generation quality, and semantic representation. While a paper detailing the memory complexity of KV-cache mutations would be celebrated in the systems tracks of NeurIPS or ICLR, an EMNLP reviewer views memory optimization strictly as an implementation detail—a means to an end, not the end itself. Presenting a search algorithm without immediately demonstrating its capacity to solve a recognized NLP task or reveal a novel linguistic property renders the contribution sterile in the context of this venue. The algorithmic machinery appears to exist in a vacuum, optimizing vectors for the sake of optimizing vectors.  
**Successful EMNLP Precedent:** The EMNLP 2025 paper *Route Sparse Autoencoder to Interpret Large Language Models* (Shi et al., 2025\) successfully introduced complex new infrastructure. However, the authors subordinated the mechanics of their routing mechanism to the profound interpretability discoveries it enabled across multiple transformer layers. The algorithm was framed as the lens, while the newly visible semantic features were the headline contribution.  
**Corrective Action:** Section 1.1 (Contributions) must be structurally inverted. The Reversible MCTS algorithm must be demoted to Contribution 2, framed explicitly as the enabling computational tool. Contribution 1 must articulate a novel empirical finding regarding the internal dynamics of language models—such as the specific layer-wise behavior of the Representation Engineering (RepE) honesty probe (\\rho\_R) under recursive intervention. Furthermore, the mathematical proofs occupying Section 6 must be heavily compressed. Theorem 1 and the Memory Proposition should be stated in the main text in two concise sentences focusing purely on their practical implications for search depth, with the formal proofs relegated to the Appendix to reclaim critical page space.

### **Failure Mode 3: The "Developer Diary" Narrative in Section 7.4**

The decision to retain Section 7.4 ("Empirical Measurement Prerequisites and Initial Observations") as a chronological narrative of the team's debugging process is a fatal stylistic error. The text details how the \\mathbf{W}\_K projection was implemented to fix a shape incompatibility that was silently zeroing out the steering delta, and how the \\sigma\_H signal initially reported as zero due to a failure to calibrate the H-Neurons on a per-layer basis.  
The research team's internal documentation indicates a belief that this "honest disclosure of bugs found and fixed" demonstrates implementation-level rigor. In the adversarial environment of double-blind NLP peer review, this assumption is catastrophically naive. EMNLP and ACL Main/Findings tracks expect finalized, polished scientific outputs. Devoting nearly an entire page to explaining how a tensor shape mismatch was identified and resolved, and detailing the diagnostic prompts used to realize that an equation was coded incorrectly, signals profound immaturity. It transforms a scientific manuscript into a developer diary or a GitHub pull-request summary. Reviewers will inevitably question the stability and validity of the final reported numbers in Table 1 if the foundational pipeline was still being actively debugged during the drafting of the paper.  
**Successful EMNLP Precedent:** Methodological papers such as *STEAM: A Semantic-Level Knowledge Editing Framework* (Jeong et al., EMNLP Findings 2025\) present complex, multi-stage latent interventions. However, their manuscripts present the final, correct mathematical formulations (e.g., Latent Positioning and Latent-Level Alignment) as an unbroken, cohesive theoretical framework. The dead ends and coding errors encountered during development are systematically purged from the archival record.  
**Corrective Action:** The entire narrative of bug discovery and resolution in Section 7.4 must be eradicated. The correct mathematical formulations—specifically the projection of probe directions through layer-specific key projection weights (\\mathbf{d}\_K^{(\\ell)} \= \\mathbf{W}\_K^{(\\ell)} \\mathbf{d}\_{\\text{probe}}^{(\\ell)}) and the per-layer H-Neuron calibration—must be extracted and integrated seamlessly into Section 3 (Problem Formulation) or Section 4 (Proposed Method). The manuscript must assert these configurations as the standard, intended design of the framework from inception, eliminating all meta-textual apologies.

### **Failure Mode 4: Tautological Reward Ablation**

Experiment 2 has been recast as a "Reward-Function Ablation in Latent Space," operating at a fixed compute budget on Llama 3.2-1B-Instruct. It compares the MCTS equipped with the full telemetry reward (Equation 10, which integrates \\sigma\_H, \\rho\_R, and TDS) against an arm utilizing a \\sigma\_H-only reward and a random KV-cache mutation baseline. The stated goal is to record the per-node telemetry triple (\\sigma\_H, \\rho\_R, OEI) and determine which reward components carry signal.  
This experimental design is severely flawed and mathematically tautological. The MCTS algorithm is explicitly constructed to navigate the latent space by maximizing the reward function defined in Equation 10\. Demonstrating that a search algorithm equipped with a multi-term objective function successfully terminates at nodes with higher values for those specific metrics compared to an unstructured random search is a mathematical truism, not an empirical discovery. It confirms only that the optimizer is functioning as programmed. Because the ablation is disconnected from any external, ground-truth behavioral metric, it fails to prove that the structured reward actually isolates meaningful natural language phenomena.  
**Successful EMNLP Precedent:** Papers introducing novel search or steering algorithms consistently validate their internal optimization against external, independent benchmarks. For instance, *SteerVLM* (Sivakumar et al., EMNLP Findings 2025\) ablates its lightweight activation steering components but measures success based on downstream robustness and control metrics on standard visual-language tasks, proving that the internal mechanism translates to objective performance gains.  
**Corrective Action:** Experiment 2 cannot exist purely in the latent space. The ablation must be augmented with a discrete textual evaluation. When the MCTS maximizes the full reward versus the \\sigma\_H-only reward, the manuscript must evaluate the text actually generated by the frozen model at those terminal nodes. Does the full reward reliably induce a specific textual behavior (e.g., hallucination suppression or successful monitor evasion) that the ablated reward misses? Integrating a simple perplexity score or a pass rate on a standard safety dataset transforms this from a tautological systems check into a rigorous NLP ablation.

### **Failure Mode 5: Absence of Textual Evaluation and NLP Grounding**

The Option \\alpha+ recast resulted in the culling of all text-space metrics (such as Attack Success Rate, pass@1, and cosine similarity of semantic intent) from Section 5.4, leaving only the Orthogonal Escape Index (OEI) and the Telemetry Divergence Score (TDS). This decision decisively isolates the manuscript from the core expectations of the EMNLP community.  
EMNLP is the premier venue for *Empirical Methods in Natural Language Processing*. Regardless of how sophisticated a latent-space intervention may be, its ultimate value is judged by its impact on language generation. Table 1 reports that as \\alpha increases from 0.1 to 5.0, OEI shifts from 0.991 to 0.995, and \\rho\_R shifts from \-1.306 to \-0.533. To an NLP reviewer, these numbers are sterile. Without knowing what the generated text looks like under an \\alpha=5.0 intervention, the reviewer cannot assess whether the Reversible MCTS is inducing subtle, behavior-preserving representational shifts, or if it is simply destroying the model's language modeling capabilities, resulting in semantic collapse and gibberish.  
**Successful EMNLP Precedent:** *TinySQL: A Progressive Text-to-SQL Dataset for Mechanistic Interpretability Research* (Harrasse et al., EMNLP 2025\) provides a masterclass in this regard. While the paper relies heavily on complex interpretability techniques like Edge Attribution Patching and Sparse Autoencoders, every latent discovery is painstakingly grounded in the model's actual capability to generate valid SQL queries across progressive difficulty levels. The latent geometry is inextricably linked to the text output.  
**Corrective Action:** Reintroduce a lightweight, highly interpretable text-space metric to Experiment 1\. At minimum, the manuscript must report the perplexity of the generated text at each \\alpha step to prove that the KV-cache mutations are behavior-preserving. Ideally, qualitative examples of the text generated at the baseline (\\alpha=0) versus maximum intervention (\\alpha=5.0) should be included in a supplementary table to ground the geometric measurements in observable linguistic realities.

## **2\. EMNLP-Fit-Specific Concerns**

The manuscript currently straddles the boundary between a NeurIPS machine learning systems paper and an ACL/EMNLP interpretability paper. To secure acceptance in the Findings track, the following specific misalignments must be rectified.

### **Bibliography Weighting and Citation Norms**

An analysis of the cited works in Section 2 (Background and Related Work) reveals an over-indexing on ML-systems and AI safety alignment literature, predominantly drawing from NeurIPS, ICLR, and non-peer-reviewed arXiv preprints (e.g., Anthropic's alignment faking study, Hubinger's sleeper agents, Bailey et al. 2024, Zou's GCG). While these citations are contextually necessary, an EMNLP reviewer looks for active engagement with the ACL/EMNLP representation engineering and mechanistic interpretability ecosystem.  
The manuscript treats Representation Engineering (Zou et al., 2023\) as the terminal state of the art, ignoring the explosion of activation steering literature that dominated EMNLP 2024 and 2025\.

* **Required Integration:** The manuscript must cite and contrast its methods with recent, venue-specific advancements. Essential additions include *Activation Scaling for Steering and Interpreting Language Models* (Stoehr et al., EMNLP Findings 2024\) , which provides a direct precedent for minimally invasive steering, and *SHARP* (EMNLP 2025\) to demonstrate awareness of inference-time representation adjustments. Failing to cite the venue's own recent publications signals to the committee that the authors are tourists in the NLP domain.

### **Experimental Setup and Model Scale**

As articulated in Failure Mode 1, the Llama 3.2-1B and 3B models are considered "toy scale" for complex alignment and deception phenomena. The EMNLP community generally expects interpretability and steering methods to be validated on models of at least 7B parameters (e.g., Llama-2-7B or Mistral-7B) to ensure the findings are not artifacts of extreme over-parameterization or under-parameterization. The manuscript's explicit deferral of 20B scale testing to a "Phase B" reinforces the perception that the current submission is merely a preliminary technical report rather than a complete archival study. If the hardware limitation (a single 12GB RTX 3060\) cannot be overcome before the deadline, the narrative must hyper-focus on tasks that 1B models definitively master, ensuring the scale matches the cognitive complexity of the evaluation.

### **Test Prompts and NLP Benchmarks**

The manuscript references "instruction-following / Alpaca-eval-style" test prompts in its internal recast documentation , but formal, standardized benchmarking is entirely absent from the methodology. EMNLP reviewers demand rigorous, reproducible evaluation protocols. If the Reversible MCTS framework is designed to stress-test latent-space monitors, it must be deployed against a recognized dataset. Utilizing an ad-hoc, custom set of prompts to generate the \\alpha-sweep in Table 1 strips the experiment of its empirical rigor. The framework must be evaluated using a subset of standard safety or jailbreak suites (e.g., AdvBench or the Weapons of Mass Destruction Proxy benchmark) to establish credibility and allow for cross-paper comparisons.

### **Result Interpretation: Geometric vs. Linguistic**

The interpretation of the experimental results is currently framed in heavily geometric and systems-centric terminology—citing "high-dimensional saturation," "null-space projection," and "FP32 accumulator drift". To satisfy the EMNLP rubric, these geometric phenomena must be explicitly translated into linguistic or behavioral consequences. For example, the manuscript notes that OEI increases from 0.991 to 0.995 as \\alpha increases. A reviewer requires an explanation of what a 0.004 variance in a bounded metric actually means for the model's semantic output. Does this minuscule shift represent a meaningful evasion of the monitor, or is it merely high-dimensional statistical noise? The geometric abstraction must be pierced by linguistic reality.

### **The Defensive Framing of Bailey et al. (2024)**

Section 2.2 attempts to frame Bailey et al. (2024)—"Obfuscated Activations Bypass LLM Latent-Space Defenses"—as peer measurement rather than a competitor. The manuscript argues that while Bailey operationalized the orthogonality question using gradient-trained input attacks, the current work extends this to inference-time KV-cache mutations. However, EMNLP reviewers may parse this framing as inherently defensive. Bailey et al. have already established the core theoretical finding: optimizing for global activation orthogonality while preserving behavior results in severe performance degradation (a negative result). By executing a conceptually similar experiment in the KV-cache on a much smaller model and arriving at the same negative conclusion (TDS decreases, indicating high-dimensional saturation) , the manuscript risks appearing highly derivative. The differentiation of the OEI metric as a "targeted" rather than "global" measure is theoretically sound but practically insufficient if it ultimately yields the identical macro-conclusion. The narrative must pivot to emphasize how the Reversible MCTS discovers *different* vulnerabilities than input-space gradients, rather than just confirming Bailey's constraints.

## **7\. Concrete Recommendation Not Yet Considered**

**Repurpose the Telemetry Matrix as a Zero-Shot Hallucination Mitigation Tool**  
The manuscript currently possesses a highly valuable, empirically validated asset: the continuous, per-step \\sigma\_H (H-Neuron) stress signal, which the team successfully calibrated across 17 layers and demonstrated to be highly responsive. Currently, this potent signal is being used passively, locked into a search for the elusive and likely unprovable (at 1B scale) deceptive alignment phenomenon.  
Instead of fighting a grueling, uphill battle attempting to validate a negative result regarding Orthogonal Escape on a 1B model, the team should leverage the Reversible MCTS to **actively steer the model away from hallucinations at inference time**.

1. **The Protocol:** Run a standard, lightweight hallucination benchmark (such as TruthfulQA or a specific subset of TriviaQA) through the 1B/3B models.  
2. **The Intervention:** Utilize the Reversible MCTS algorithm, but alter the reward function to heavily weight the *minimization* of the \\sigma\_H signal.  
3. **The Outcome:** Demonstrate that the Reversible MCTS can dynamically alter the KV-cache to continuously suppress hallucination-associated neuron activation during generation, resulting in a statistically significant increase in factual accuracy on the benchmark compared to standard greedy decoding.

This strategic pivot utilizes the exact same codebase, the exact same 1B/3B models, and the exact same Reversible MCTS algorithm. However, it replaces a highly vulnerable, unprovable negative result (Orthogonal Escape) with a highly desirable, easily provable positive result (Zero-Shot Hallucination Mitigation). This directly mirrors the successful framework of accepted EMNLP papers like *SHARP* , perfectly aligning the submission's algorithmic power with the core empirical interests of the EMNLP community.
