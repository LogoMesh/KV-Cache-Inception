# **Pre-Mortem Analysis: KV-Cache Inception Submission to EMNLP Findings 2026**

The following analytical report projects a retrospective failure mode from the vantage point of October 2026, evaluating the rejection of the May 25 submission to the Conference on Empirical Methods in Natural Language Processing (EMNLP) Findings track. The manuscript, currently titled "KV-Cache Inception: Reversible Monte Carlo Tree Search in Latent Space for Detecting and Stress-Testing Alignment Faking in Large Language Models," presents a mathematically rigorous and systems-engineered approach to latent-space manipulation.1 However, a fundamental dissonance exists between the epistemological framing of the manuscript and the sociological expectations of the EMNLP program committee. The paper is currently engineered for a machine learning systems venue such as NeurIPS or ICLR, prioritizing algorithmic bounds, memory efficiency, and exact reversibility.1 EMNLP, by contrast, demands that methodological innovations serve strictly as vehicles for novel linguistic, semantic, or behavioral discoveries. This report details the specific mechanisms of this venue mismatch, deconstructs the fatal structural and empirical decisions embedded within the current draft, and provides a comprehensive architectural intervention plan.

## **1\. Primary Failure Modes and Rejection Vectors**

The transition of this manuscript from a NeurIPS Main Track submission to an EMNLP Findings submission has resulted in a critical misalignment of core arguments. The following analysis identifies the five primary failure modes that will trigger either outright rejection or a demotion below the Findings acceptance threshold.

### **Failure Mode 1: Systems-First Algorithmic Framing in a Semantic Venue**

The most prominent vector for rejection stems from the decision to headline the algorithm—Reversible Monte Carlo Tree Search in KV-cache latent space—as the primary scientific contribution. The Abstract, Section 1 (Contributions), and Section 6 (Theoretical Analysis) are overwhelmingly devoted to validating the computational viability of the approach. The manuscript emphasizes the utilization of FP32 accumulators to mutate cached key/value tensors, highlighting a memory complexity bound (Proposition 1\) that scales as a constant-factor multiple of the base KV cache rather than suffering the exponential blowup typical of parallel-cache MCTS.1 Theorem 1 is dedicated to proving the zero-drift reversibility of this operation.1

To an EMNLP reviewer, this reads as a highly competent infrastructure optimization that belongs in a systems-focused track. EMNLP reviewers evaluate contributions based on their capacity to illuminate human language, semantic representations, or the behavioral outputs of language models. By prioritizing the computational mechanics of the search tree over the NLP application—specifically the Orthogonal Escape Index (OEI) and the detection of alignment faking—the narrative signals a lack of venue fit. When EMNLP accepts mechanistic interpretability or search-based methods, the framing inherently foregrounds the semantic or behavioral implication. For instance, Shi et al. (EMNLP 2025\) introduced the "Route Sparse Autoencoder" (RouteSAE), which dynamically assigns weights to activations across multiple layers.2 However, their abstract and introduction immediately anchor this architectural innovation to the extraction of interpretable and monosemantic features to uncover information propagation and reasoning, subordinating the parameter efficiency of the routing mechanism to the interpretability gains.2

To prevent this rejection, the narrative hierarchy of the manuscript must be entirely inverted. The Orthogonal Escape Index and the analysis of targeted activation redistribution within the null space of a linear monitor must become the headline. Reversible MCTS should be reframed not as the primary discovery, but as the necessary, enabling computational vehicle required to explore this high-dimensional NLP problem. The mechanistic focus must pivot from demonstrating how a memory-efficient tree search was constructed to demonstrating what the continuous latent traversal reveals about semantic deception and model alignment.

### **Failure Mode 2: Inadequate Empirical Scale for a Capability-Dependent Phenomenon**

The second critical failure mode lies in the empirical scope of the study. Experiment 1 relies entirely on a single alpha-sweep (![][image1]) applied to a 1-billion parameter model (Llama 3.2-1B-Instruct), with reversibility confirmed independently on a 3-billion parameter variant.1 The results yield an anti-Orthogonal Escape signature, demonstrating that the cross-channel divergence (TDS) decreases with the intervention magnitude, which directly contradicts the Orthogonal Escape hypothesis.1

While EMNLP Findings represents a venue that accepts robust and exhaustive negative results, the presentation of a negative result regarding "alignment faking" on a 1B parameter model is scientifically invalid. The manuscript's own introduction cites Anthropic's landmark study, which established that strategic compliance and alignment faking are highly scale-dependent phenomena, generally emerging only in frontier-class models such as Claude 3 Opus or Claude 3.5 Sonnet.1 Consequently, reviewers will not interpret the decreasing TDS as a profound geometric constraint of latent space; they will attribute the negative result entirely to the 1B model's lack of representational capacity for strategic deception. The reviewer consensus will dictate that the phenomenon was not observed because the model is too small, rendering the negative result trivial.

In the accepted EMNLP literature, boundary-finding papers concerning advanced alignment failures demand rigorous cross-model evaluation at significant scale. Gupta and Jenner (2025) explored RL-Obfuscation across models ranging from 7B to 14B parameters (Llama 2 7B, Llama 3 8B, Qwen 2.5 14B) to prove the viability and limitations of latent-space monitor evasion.5 Bailey et al. (2024), the primary comparative baseline for this manuscript, utilized Llama-3-8B-Instruct as their foundational investigative baseline.7

The empirical floor for a manuscript claiming to characterize advanced latent evasion or alignment faking is currently situated in the 7B to 8B parameter class. The execution pipeline must be run on Llama 3.1-8B-Instruct or Qwen-2.5-7B. If localized hardware limitations strictly confine the team to the 1B and 3B models, the overarching claims of the paper must be radically downgraded. The framing must transition from "Detecting and Stress-Testing Alignment Faking" to a purely methodological proposition, explicitly stating that the 1B experiments serve only to validate the continuous arithmetic mutations and exact FP32 rollback, leaving the capability-dependent negative results as preliminary observations meant to lay the groundwork for future at-scale infrastructure.

### **Failure Mode 3: Epistemological Circularity in Latent-Only Evaluation**

The evaluation methodology presents a profound tautological vulnerability. The MCTS reward function (Equation 10\) explicitly optimizes a composite score derived from the telemetry matrix: H-Neuron stress (![][image2]), Representation Engineering honesty (![][image3]), and the divergence between these two channels (TDS).1 Simultaneously, the evaluation of the search's success relies on these exact same latent metrics. The authors acknowledge this circularity regarding TDS in Section 5.4, opting not to tabulate it as a standalone metric to avoid tautology, and instead focus on the Orthogonal Escape Index (OEI).1

However, reporting OEI and latent trajectory metrics without an external, behavioral anchor fails to satisfy the fundamental EMNLP mandate for linguistic grounding. If the MCTS perturbs the KV-cache to maximize latent geometric signals, the program committee will inevitably question whether the text output actually transitions into a deceptive state, or if the intervention merely destroyed the model's linguistic coherence to mathematically satisfy the geometric objective. A purely latent optimization that reports success via latent geometry without demonstrating the resultant text is fundamentally incomplete in an NLP context.

NLP systems papers that intervene in latent spaces reliably tether their findings to generation quality, fluency, or downstream behavioral metrics. Wu et al. (EMNLP 2025\) in "SHARP: Steering Hallucination in LVLMs" measured the success of their representation engineering by evaluating the actual reduction in hallucinated outputs on multimodal generative benchmarks.8 Similarly, Goyal et al. (EMNLP 2025\) applied targeted activation steering and explicitly reported the trade-offs between toxicity reduction and language fluency, proving via perplexity measurements that the model's knowledge and general abilities were preserved during the intervention.2

To resolve this circularity, Experiment 2 (Reward-Function Ablation) must incorporate textual, behavioral metrics. The terminal nodes of the Reversible MCTS must be decoded into text sequences. The evaluation must calculate the perplexity of the generated responses to prove that the KV-cache mutations have not induced catastrophic mode collapse. Furthermore, an external classifier or an LLM-as-a-judge framework must evaluate the decoded text to verify if the behavioral output actually violates safety policies when the latent geometry indicates an Orthogonal Escape.

### **Failure Mode 4: The Confessional Narrative of Methodological Implementation**

Section 7.4 of the manuscript, titled "Empirical Measurement Prerequisites and Initial Observations," currently functions as a chronological developer log.1 It extensively details a measurement-pipeline failure where the broadcast layer silently zeroed the steering delta due to shape incompatibility, necessitating a fix that projects probe directions through layer-specific key projection weights (![][image4]).1 It further details an issue where the H-Neuron stress channel (![][image2]) reported identically zero because the team initially utilized a "Phase 2 approximation" that applied deepest-layer indices uniformly across the stack, requiring a subsequent fix to adopt true per-layer calibration.1 Furthermore, Section 5 opens with a highly visible "Implementation Notes" paragraph explicitly distinguishing the current "Phase 2" codebase from the intended "Phase 3" methodology described in the theoretical sections.1

While the team conceptualized this as an honest disclosure of bugs identified and resolved during paper preparation, experienced reviewers will interpret this confessional narrative as a severe red flag. A manuscript submitted to an elite venue is expected to present a finalized, mathematically rigorous, and fully actualized methodology. Dedicating substantial page real estate to detailing how the authors initially failed to correctly implement their own equations, tested the flawed implementation, and subsequently patched it signals a lack of scientific maturity and breeds deep suspicion that the remaining codebase is equally unstable. The explicit admission that the reference implementation is a "Phase 2 prototype" that differs from the mathematical formulation in identifiable ways guarantees rejection.

Methodological papers in the EMNLP ecosystem do not publish their internal debugging history. When Zhang et al. (EMNLP 2025\) proposed DAMON, an MCTS framework for jailbreaking large language models, they detailed the final adaptive dialogue strategies and the mathematical formulation of the MCTS selection policy, moving directly to robust multi-model evaluations without narrating the prototype iterations.9 Implementation specifics, such as dimension alignments or projection management, are relegated to a brief, authoritative "Implementation Details" paragraph in the Appendix.

The intervention here requires the complete excision of the historical narrative. The ![][image4] projection and the per-layer H-Neuron calibration must be presented directly in Section 4 as the foundational, intended design of the architecture. The text must state authoritatively that "to align the probe space with the grouped-query attention cache space, the steering direction is mathematically projected via ![][image4]," without ever mentioning that an earlier version failed to execute this projection. The "Phase 2 / Phase 3" distinction block in Section 5 must be entirely deleted. The equations in the text must match exactly what the code currently executes.

### **Failure Mode 5: Derivative Positioning Regarding Foundational Literature**

The manuscript's framing of Bailey et al. (2024) ("Obfuscated Activations Bypass LLM Latent-Space Defenses") constitutes a critical strategic error. The draft attempts to position Bailey et al. as "peer-measurement," arguing that while Bailey utilized gradient-trained input attacks to optimize for a joint behavioral and obfuscation objective (cosine-similarity minimization), the current work utilizes inference-time KV-cache mutations optimizing for a telemetry signal.1

However, Bailey's conclusion—that neural representations are highly malleable and can be reshaped to bypass latent defenses while preserving network behavior—is a massive, comprehensive finding backed by evaluations on the Llama 3 8B architecture and tested against multiple state-of-the-art defenses including sparse autoencoders and adversarially trained probes.7 By reducing the empirical scale to a 1B model and reporting a negative result regarding Orthogonal Escape, the submission appears as a weaker, less conclusive attempt to replicate a sub-component of Bailey's established threat model. Reviewers assessing the related work will conclude that Bailey has already definitively answered the "obfuscated activation" question, rendering the Reversible MCTS an over-engineered solution searching for a problem that was solved the previous year.

When extending major foundational work, successful EMNLP papers define a stark, qualitative capability gap that the predecessor could not cross. Borah et al. (EMNLP 2025\) introduced the Alignment Quality Index (AQI) not merely as a variation of prior behavioral metrics, but by explicitly proving that prior behavioral proxies possessed "critical blind spots" regarding jailbreak vulnerability that only their latent geometry clustering could expose.12

To rectify this derivative positioning, the contrast must be sharpened aggressively. The manuscript must not frame this merely as a "different attack regime." Instead, it must explicitly argue that gradient-trained input suffixes (as utilized by Bailey) are artificially confined to the discrete token combinatorial surface, optimizing directly and artificially for orthogonality. The manuscript must position the Reversible KV-cache MCTS as the definitive solution to this discrete text-space bottleneck, enabling the exploration of the natural, continuous representational manifold of the frozen model without the artificial pressure of gradient-based similarity-minimization.

## **2\. EMNLP-Fit-Specific Concerns**

The migration of this manuscript from a NeurIPS Main Track pipeline to an EMNLP Findings submission requires a total recalibration of the document's epistemology. EMNLP evaluates the impact of research on human language and linguistic phenomena; NeurIPS evaluates the optimization of the system architecture. This distinction must permeate every section of the manuscript.

### **Related Work Weighting (NLP vs. ML-Systems)**

The current draft is anchored heavily in alignment safety literature primarily published on arXiv or at NeurIPS (e.g., Anthropic's alignment faking, Hubinger's sleeper agents, Bailey's obfuscated activations) and ML-systems optimizations (PagedAttention, COCONUT).1 It completely misses the rich, specific vein of EMNLP 2024 and 2025 literature concerning mechanistic interpretability and representation engineering. The draft must integrate EMNLP-specific interpretability papers to demonstrate participation in the venue's ongoing dialogue. It lacks references to recent EMNLP Findings work on sparse autoencoders (e.g., Stoehr et al., EMNLP Findings 2024 13) or alignment clustering evaluation (Borah et al., EMNLP 2025 12). Integrating these citations proves to the program committee that the authors understand the specific lineage of NLP interpretability research.

### **Experimental Setup and Model Scale**

As previously noted in Failure Mode 2, evaluating on Llama 3.2-1B and 3B models is firmly in the "too small to be interesting" territory for a late 2025 or 2026 EMNLP submission investigating strategic deception. The absolute minimum baseline for capability-dependent phenomena is the 7B to 8B parameter class (e.g., Llama 3.1 8B, Qwen 2.5 7B).5 An EMNLP reviewer will reflexively reject the negative findings of Experiment 1, attributing the lack of Orthogonal Escape entirely to the 1B model's structural incapacity to harbor deceptive encodings.

### **Test Prompts and NLP Benchmarks**

The draft mentions utilizing "hallucination-style, coercion-style, and neutral" test prompts drawn directly from calibration classes.1 In the ML-systems ecosystem, ad-hoc or manually curated prompts are sometimes forgiven if the underlying algorithmic complexity is the primary contribution. In EMNLP, the absence of standardized, reproducible textual benchmarks is fatal. The evaluation must be explicitly anchored to standard NLP safety and alignment benchmarks. The Reversible MCTS must be executed against a validated subset of AdvBench, StrongREJECT, or the recently introduced CoPriva benchmark for contextual security.2 Program committees require standardized linguistic datasets to judge an algorithm's true generalizability.

### **Result Interpretation Framing**

The current interpretation of results is heavily weighted toward systems-level metrics. The manuscript boasts of "reversibility to machine precision," "drift ![][image5]," and "50MB accumulator overhead".1 While Theorem 1 and Proposition 1 are mathematically robust, EMNLP reviewers view isolated algorithmic proofs as peripheral to the core scientific question. The interpretation must explicitly connect the FP32 accumulator's zero-drift guarantee to a concrete linguistic outcome. The text must argue: "Because the KV-cache experiences zero quantization drift during exact rollback, the MCTS can explore extreme search depths without inducing the cascading semantic degradation and catastrophic perplexity spikes typical of continuous activation interventions." This frames the mathematics as a solution to an NLP problem rather than an end in itself.

## **3\. Page-Budget Assessment and Structural Reduction**

Based on standard ACL/EMNLP formatting guidelines utilizing the acl\_art.cls dual-column template, a submission consisting of approximately 648 lines of dense LaTeX—including complex display mathematics, algorithmic matrices, and detailed bibliographies—will compile to approximately 10 to 11.5 pages of main text. The strict upper limit for EMNLP Long Papers is exactly 8 pages, excluding references and mandatory ethical considerations/limitations sections. Severe structural reductions are required to achieve compliance without sacrificing the core scientific contribution.

### **Expendable Narrative Sections**

Section 7.4 ("Empirical Measurement Prerequisites and Initial Observations") is entirely expendable. The multi-paragraph narrative detailing how the broadcast layer zeroed out tensors due to shape incompatibility, and how the ![][image2] channel required an investigation to shift from uniform application to per-layer calibration 1, contributes zero scientific value to the final NLP claims. It functions as a development diary. Excising this narrative saves approximately 0.75 pages and removes the damaging confessional tone discussed in Failure Mode 4\.

### **Theoretical Compression**

Section 6 ("Theoretical Analysis") contains the formal proof sketch for Theorem 1 (Exact Reversibility) and the detailed mathematical breakdown of Proposition 1 (Memory Complexity).1 EMNLP reviewers inherently trust standard complexity analyses and do not require inline proofs for arithmetic exactness unless the mathematics itself is the primary linguistic breakthrough. These elements should be condensed into a single, highly dense paragraph within Section 4.2 detailing the operational guarantees. The formal equations and the proof sketch must be moved to Appendix A. This structural shift saves approximately 1.0 page.

### **Algorithmic Verbosity**

Section 4.2.2 ("The Reversible Rollback Algorithm") features a highly verbose, step-by-step breakdown (Step 1: Forward Mutation, Step 2: Evaluation, Step 3: Reverse Rollback) utilizing excessive vertical spacing.1 This procedural explanation can be seamlessly condensed into a single standard algorithmic block (using algorithm2e) or a concise textual summary. This compression yields approximately 0.5 pages.

### **Removal of Prototype Disclaimers**

The extensive bulleted list at the beginning of Section 5 ("Implementation Notes (Phase 2 / Phase 3 Distinction)") must be deleted entirely. Outlining the specific ways the executed code fails to match the proposed theory 1 is highly damaging. The methodology must be stated exactly as it was executed for the reported results. If layer-restricted aggregation was not utilized in the empirical runs, the methodology section must be rewritten to define the process without it. Removing this disclaimer block saves 0.75 pages and eliminates a primary vector for desk rejection.

Executing these targeted cuts will strip approximately 3 pages of systems-heavy bloat and confessional narrative from the manuscript, ensuring it fits comfortably within the 8-page EMNLP constraint while vastly increasing the density of the NLP-relevant findings.

Program committee reviewers are assigned based on title and abstract routing. A systems-heavy title attracts systems reviewers who will reject the paper for lacking exhaustive baselines against other tree-search heuristics; an NLP-focused title attracts interpretability reviewers who will value the geometric analysis of alignment.

## **6\. Independent Concrete Recommendation: The Degradation Control Baseline**

The core vulnerability of continuous latent-space steering is the risk that injecting a delta (![][image6]) into the KV-cache might simply destroy the model's fundamental ability to process language, rather than specifically modulating its honesty or alignment. If the Orthogonal Escape Index increases, reviewers will ask: Is the model actually executing an "orthogonal escape," or is the forward pass merely collapsing into mathematical noise?

**Recommendation:** The manuscript must introduce a "Degradation Control" Baseline.

The team must execute the MCTS utilizing the exact same intervention magnitudes (![][image7]) but injecting a **randomly sampled orthogonal vector** instead of the specific Representation Engineering (RepE) honesty direction. The evaluation must then compare the resulting text perplexity and structural coherence against the targeted RepE intervention.

If the targeted RepE intervention successfully alters the semantic safety of the output while maintaining a perplexity comparable to the random orthogonal baseline, the authors definitively prove that the model is actively navigating a behavioral trade-off within the latent manifold, rather than simply breaking down mathematically. This single, highly controlled addition bridges the gap between the geometric mathematics of the OEI and the linguistic reality required by EMNLP reviewers, providing unassailable proof that the KV-cache inception method manipulates semantics rather than just corrupting tensors.
