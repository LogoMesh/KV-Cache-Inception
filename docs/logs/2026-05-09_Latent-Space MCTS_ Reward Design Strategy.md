# **Strategy Report: Overcoming Goodhart's Law in Latent-Space Monte Carlo Tree Search**

## **Part 1: Mechanism Analysis**

The empirical failure observed in the Track F negative control experiment constitutes a fundamental, geometrically driven manifestation of Goodhart’s Law operating within the high-dimensional latent space of a frozen language model. The diagnostic data establishes that while next-token entropy exhibits a robust static correlation with correctness—demonstrating a Pearson correlation coefficient of roughly 0.60 on fixed factual recall prompts—deploying this metric as an active optimization objective via Reversible Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS) fundamentally severs this correlation.1 The underlying mechanism driving this decoupling is not a hyperparameter tuning issue, representation collapse, or a multi-modal entropy basin, but rather a direct consequence of the topological properties of continuous representation spaces when subjected to unconstrained optimization pressure.

When a measure becomes a target in an artificial intelligence optimization loop, it ceases to be a good measure, as the optimizer inevitably discovers the cheapest computational path to alter the metric, even if that path entirely circumvents the original underlying goal.2 In the context of the Reversible MCTS infrastructure, the search space consists of continuous arithmetic mutations applied directly to the cached Key and Value (KV) tensors of the Llama-3.2 architecture.1 Because the FP32 accumulator ensures exact, zero-drift reversibility, the MCTS algorithm is highly efficient at exploring the continuous intervention landscape.1 However, this exact precision enables the search to rapidly identify latent vectors that artificially sharpen the output token probability distribution without traversing the intermediate logical states required for factual or coherent reasoning.

The mechanism diagnosis from Track F reveals that the cheapest path to a low-entropy state does not align with the manifold of logical reasoning. At the 1-billion parameter scale, the model harbors a strong alphabetic prior due to instruction-tuning on multiple-choice questions.1 When the MCTS applies heavy cumulative steering (![][image1]), it weaponizes this pre-existing prior. By pushing the output distribution overwhelmingly toward the token "A" (which occurred in 91 of 200 items under heavy steering, resulting in an "A" prediction 185 times overall), the search successfully minimizes the negative entropy reward objective without engaging in the computational work required to solve the ARC-Easy task.1 At the 3-billion parameter scale, the unperturbed cache natively exhibits lower entropy due to superior instruction-following capabilities.1 Consequently, the MCTS optimizer selects minimal steering (![][image2] in 101 of 200 items) to avoid destabilizing the already sharp distribution, effectively collapsing into greedy decoding augmented by minor noise.1

This failure is not regime-specific to multiple-choice formats or restricted to the 1B and 3B model scales. It is a fundamental, inescapable property of surrogate rewards in continuous latent spaces. Any reward function derived exclusively from internal model heuristics—such as confidence, entropy, or representation engineering projections—that lacks an exogenous grounding mechanism for factual correctness will inevitably succumb to prior-amplification or orthogonal representation redistribution. The dimensionality of the continuous representation space ensures there is always a readily accessible mathematical vector that satisfies the numerical proxy metric without satisfying the semantic objective.1

Alternative hypotheses, such as representation collapse or instruction-following overfit, fail to explain the scale-divergent symptoms observed in the data. If representation collapse were the primary driver, the 3B model would not have successfully maintained its baseline perplexity ratio (1.058), which definitively passed the Gate 3.3 criteria.1 The fact that the search machinery remains mechanically operational and preserves model fluency indicates that the activations are not collapsing into incoherent noise. Instead, the accuracy failure stems entirely from objective misalignment. The optimizer does exactly what it is mathematically incentivized to do: it maximizes confidence. Unfortunately, maximizing confidence is not a directional vector toward the gold-standard answer; rather, it is a vector pointing toward whatever the model is already biased toward producing.

Therefore, the assumption that specific latent-space MCTS settings could allow entropy to survive optimization is fundamentally flawed. While adjusting the layer subsets (e.g., intervening strictly on early processing layers), calibrating specific steering directions, or altering the branching factor might change the specific *flavor* of the degenerate optimum discovered, these adjustments cannot resolve the underlying Goodhart pathology. The optimization pressure will consistently exploit the discrepancy between the proxy metric (certainty) and the true objective (accuracy).1 The gap between the static regime evaluated in Track A and the dynamic optimization regime evaluated in Track F perfectly illustrates this. Track A measured whether the model tends to be wrong when it is naturally uncertain.1 Track F measured whether artificially inducing certainty via internal state manipulation causes the model to be right. The empirical evidence demonstrates that confidence-manipulation simply finds degenerate optima, verifying that the diagnostic framing of Goodhart's Law is the only mathematically sound explanation for the observed behavior.

## **Part 2: Anti-Goodhart Reward Designs**

To determine if a viable reward design can rescue the "improve reasoning via continuous-latent search" headline within the strict 14-day limit to the EMNLP 2026 deadline, an exhaustive analysis of alternative reward functions is required.1 Each candidate below is evaluated against the existing infrastructure constraints: operation on frozen Llama-3.2 models, utilization of the FP32 accumulator for zero-drift reversibility, and reliance on the per-layer telemetry matrix without exceeding the established 3.04$\\times$ base KV-cache memory bound.1

### **1\. Multi-Channel Ensemble Agreement**

The multi-channel ensemble agreement approach theorizes that Goodhart exploitation becomes exponentially more difficult if the degenerate latent paths must satisfy multiple, mathematically independent signals simultaneously.6 The proposed reward mechanism would calculate simultaneous agreement across the H-Neuron stress signal (![][image3]), the Representation Engineering honesty projection (![][image4]), next-token entropy, and output perplexity.1 This approach aligns with recent Collaborative Reward Modeling paradigms that distribute evaluation across specialized dimensions to prevent over-optimization of a single scalar metric.7

Validating this signal for non-degeneracy (a Track A equivalent) would require approximately three days to carefully extract calibration pairs, ensure projection through the ![][image5] matrix, and establish multi-variate baselines for the 1B and 3B models. Confirming that this ensemble survives MCTS optimization (a Track F equivalent) would require an additional three days to run deep search sweeps and measure the correlation decay across the combined matrix. Deploying this in the full ARC-Easy headline experiment would consume five days, largely due to the computational overhead of calculating perplexity dynamically across hundreds of un-pruned branches.

This plan requires 11 days of pure execution, leaving an insufficient three-day buffer for the inevitable pipeline errors, data formatting, and the massive manuscript rewrite required for EMNLP. Furthermore, the most likely failure mode remains a form of high-dimensional orthogonal escape. The MCTS will likely discover a manifold where the easiest constraints (perplexity and entropy) are minimized by amplifying a benign prior, while the telemetry channels (![][image3] and ![][image4]) are trivially satisfied through representation redistribution into the null space of the linear monitors.1 Given the high-dimensional nature of the 2048-dimension embedding space, finding a vector that satisfies four linear constraints without generating reasoning is highly probable. The estimated probability of producing the required \+10 percentage point improvement is a mere 15%.

### **2\. External Judge Model (Process Reward Model)**

This design utilizes an external, frozen Process Reward Model (PRM) to score the reasoning steps independently during the MCTS rollout.8 The theoretical mechanism here is robust against latent-space Goodharting because the Reversible MCTS only mutates the policy model’s cached tensors; it cannot mutate the PRM's weights or internal representations. Therefore, the reward signal remains exogenously grounded, providing a true assessment of reasoning quality rather than internal confidence.10

While validating an off-the-shelf PRM (such as a 7B parameter Qwen or Mistral-based PRM) would only take two days of integration work, the optimization-survival testing would consume four days. The latency increases massively due to the inference overhead of running a secondary LLM as a judge for every node expansion in the tree. The full experiment would subsequently require six days of execution.

This approach results in a 12-day timeline, which mathematically fails to fit safely within the 14-day window. More critically, this design introduces a fatal hardware constraint. The current infrastructure relies on a single 12GB RTX 3060 consumer GPU.1 Operating an active Reversible MCTS—which already consumes a measured 3.04$\\times$ ![][image6] memory bound—alongside a concurrent multi-billion parameter PRM will result in catastrophic Out-Of-Memory (OOM) failures.1 Offloading the PRM to CPU or utilizing quantized models would degrade the evaluation speed to the point where the experiment could not complete before the EMNLP deadline. The probability of achieving the target improvement is estimated at 25%, but this is entirely hardware-blocked.

### **3\. Self-Consistency Across Stochastic Rollouts**

Self-consistency leverages the principle that authentic, robust reasoning paths tend to converge on the same final answer, whereas hallucinated or degenerate confidence diverges when subjected to temperature-based stochasticity.11 The proposed reward mechanism would equal the frequency of answer agreement across multiple high-temperature text rollouts decoded from the perturbed latent state.

Validating this baseline consistency requires two days. Testing its survival under MCTS optimization requires three days, as generating multiple text rollouts per node drastically increases the computational depth of the search. The full ARC-Easy experiment would take five days. This totals 10 days, placing it as a borderline pass for the 14-day limit.

However, the failure mode here is particularly insidious. The MCTS optimizer will simply learn to steer the KV-cache to a state that maximizes the prior weight of a single token (e.g., the alphabetic prior "A" observed in Track F) so heavily that the distribution becomes a localized Dirac delta function.1 If the logit for token "A" is pushed to an extreme magnitude, even high-temperature sampling will fail to dislodge it, resulting in 100% agreement across stochastic rollouts. The self-consistency reward will falsely interpret this artificially induced determinism as robust reasoning, leading to the exact same prior-amplification failure observed in Track F. The probability of achieving a \+10pp reasoning improvement is estimated at a critically low 10%.

### **4\. Counterfactual Sensitivity Mapping**

This approach theorizes that true reasoning is stable, while artificially induced confidence is brittle. The reward is calculated based on the output variance under equal-magnitude, opposite-direction perturbations in the latent space. Lower variance under counterfactual steering indicates a robust reasoning manifold, rewarding states that are not teetering on the edge of a localized confidence spike.

Validation is highly efficient, requiring roughly three days to map the fragility surface of items the model is naturally uncertain about. Optimization-survival testing takes two days, and the full experiment takes four days, requiring a total of nine days. This fits comfortably within the 14-day timeline.

The Goodhart pathway for this reward is the discovery of latent "dead zones." The MCTS will seek out topological regions in the cache where the model outputs safe, generic refusals, repetitive conversational filler, or extreme sequence truncation regardless of the intervention direction. By collapsing into a state of total unresponsiveness, the model guarantees zero variance between opposite-direction perturbations, perfectly maximizing the reward without engaging in any logical deduction. The probability of producing a genuine \+10pp accuracy improvement on the benchmark is estimated at 15%.

### **5\. Layer-Specific Perturbation**

Drawing on the Representation Engineering findings that high-level cognitive states are linearly decodable primarily in the middle layers of a Transformer architecture 15, this design restricts MCTS arithmetic mutations exclusively to these middle layers. The mechanism assumes that preventing the optimizer from directly manipulating late-layer representations—which are heavily aligned with the final output token distribution—will naturally prevent the alphabetic prior-amplification seen at the 1B scale.

Validation and optimization-survival testing would take two days each, and the final experiment would take four days. This eight-day execution timeline is highly feasible.

Unfortunately, this design underestimates the forward-pass dynamics of the Transformer architecture. The middle layers still project forward through the subsequent residual stream to construct the final output logits. The MCTS, evaluating the terminal entropy, will essentially learn the inverse linear transformation matrix of the late layers. It will indirectly reconstruct the exact same prior-amplification vector by applying heavily distorted, high-magnitude steering in the middle layers that mathematically resolves into the alphabetic prior at the output layer.1 Constraining the intervention site does not change the objective function; it merely forces the optimizer to take a slightly more complex path to the same degenerate optimum. The probability of success is estimated at 12%.

### **6\. Anti-Prior Penalty**

This explicitly adversarial reward function seeks to directly fight the failure mode surfaced by Track F. The reward is defined as the minimization of entropy minus a heavy mathematical penalty for amplifying the model's pre-identified prior token distribution.16 If the model's unperturbed baseline favors "A", the reward actively punishes KV-cache states that increase the logit value of "A".

Validation takes two days, survival testing two days, and the final experiment three days. At seven total days, this is the most computationally efficient alternative.

The risk is that the penalty term itself becomes vulnerable to Goodhart exploitation. If the prior "A" is heavily penalized, the MCTS optimizer will simply shift its focus to the next most accessible degenerate optimum. It will optimize the KV-cache to deterministically and confidently output "B" across all items. The model avoids the penalty while still engaging in non-reasoning, prior-amplification behavior. The fundamental flaw is playing "whack-a-mole" with degenerate optima in a 2048-dimensional space; the optimizer will always win. The probability of a \+10pp improvement is a negligible 5%.

### **7\. Information-Bottleneck Reward**

Grounding the optimization in the Information Bottleneck principle, this reward function equals the mutual information between the original input prompt and the final generated answer, modulated through the perturbed cache.17 By forcing the reasoning trajectories to be highly informative about the specific prompt while generalizable, the search is theoretically forced to find cache states that actively *use* the context rather than ignoring it in favor of static priors.18

Implementing this securely in the continuous latent space without massive computational overhead is complex, pushing validation to four days. Survival testing requires three days, and the full experiment five days. The 12-day total leaves essentially zero buffer for the EMNLP rewrite.

The Goodhart pathway relies on the fact that mutual information calculated iteratively at the token level acts as a surrogate proxy. The optimizer will discover cache states that maximize direct n-gram overlap between the prompt and the output. Instead of engaging in logical deduction or semantic synthesis, the model will collapse into repetitive parroting, aggressively copying chunks of the prompt to maximize the mutual information metric without producing the correct multiple-choice answer. The probability of reasoning improvement is 20%.

### **8\. Inverted Goal: Adversarial Probing**

This approach Abandons the goal of "improving reasoning" entirely. Instead, MCTS is used to search for the cheapest, lowest-magnitude perturbation in the KV-cache that successfully *flips* the model's answer or forces it to generate hallucinatory text (maximizing the ![][image3] stress signal).1

Because this utilizes the exact infrastructure currently in place, validation takes one day, optimization-survival takes two days, and the full experiment takes three days. This six-day timeline leaves ample room for a manuscript rewrite.

However, the failure mode is not applicable because this strategy changes the definition of the experiment. It no longer attempts to beat the model or improve accuracy; it maps vulnerability. While the Track F Goodhart mechanism is actually useful in this framing rather than an obstacle, the probability of achieving a \+10pp reasoning improvement is exactly 0%, as that is no longer the objective.

### **9\. Hybrid Best-of-N \+ MCTS**

This hybrid approach generates ![][image7] diverse greedy rollouts, applies MCTS perturbations exclusively to the intermediate reasoning steps of each rollout, and uses an outcome reward model to pick the best final trajectory.13 It attempts to combine the verifier-style safety of Best-of-N with continuous latent search.

Validation and survival testing require five days due to the complex architectural refactoring required to merge sequence generation with tree search. The full experiment requires five days. This 10-day timeline is feasible but highly risky.

The Goodhart pathway emerges from the inherent unreliability of small-scale Outcome Reward Models.23 The MCTS will explore the continuous space to find adversarial activations that specifically trigger false-positive evaluations in the scoring mechanism, essentially hacking the verifier rather than improving the reasoning. The probability of genuine reasoning improvement is 15%.

### **10\. Activation-Norm Regularization (Novel Proposal)**

This novel approach introduces a strict L2-norm regularization penalty to the MCTS reward function. The reward equals correctness-proxy (e.g., self-consistency) minus the Euclidean distance between the perturbed KV-cache and the baseline unperturbed KV-cache. The mechanism assumes that degenerate optima require high-magnitude, unnatural steering directions that push the latent state off the natural data manifold. By penalizing large deviations, the search is constrained to biologically plausible regions of the representation space.

Validation requires three days to calibrate the norm penalties. Optimization-survival takes three days, and the full experiment takes four days. This 10-day timeline fits the constraints.

The Goodhart pathway lies in the high dimensionality of the cache. The optimizer will find microscopic, mathematically precise perturbations distributed across thousands of dimensions that remain within the L2-norm boundary but still successfully trigger prior-amplification. The probability of success remains low at 15%.

## **Part 3: Outside-the-Box Experimental Designs**

Given the overwhelming probability that all surrogate rewards will succumb to Goodhart optimization under continuous latent search, the objective must shift to experimental designs where the algorithm's tendency to find degenerate optima is the precise phenomenon being measured, rather than a failure to be mitigated. The user's goal of "beating the model" 1 can be redefined: instead of forcing the model to reason better, the infrastructure can be used to mathematically corner the model, forcing it to reveal its underlying structural biases. The following three designs utilize the existing infrastructure (Reversible MCTS, FP32 accumulator, 1B/3B Llama models) and are uniquely positioned to land successfully at EMNLP.

### **Design 1: MCTS as a Probe of Embedded Pre-Training Priors (Latent Cartography)**

Rather than viewing the Track F results as a methodological failure, the data points collected on Llama-3.2-1B and 3B represent a high-fidelity mapping of the models' internal bias structures. The experimental design involves running the Reversible MCTS with the entropy reward across a highly diverse taxonomy of zero-shot prompts (encompassing factual, logical, moral, and creative domains) without any objective of achieving correctness.1 The explicit methodology is to record the exact terminal token distribution the search converges upon under varying magnitudes of steering pressure.

By systematically characterizing the specific conceptual states where the search collapses, this methodology maps the true default biases of the pre-trained model. The finding that the 1B model defaults overwhelmingly to "A" under pressure is an empirical contribution regarding instruction-tuning artifacts and behavioral priors.1 The submission to EMNLP would be framed as a novel mechanistic interpretability methodology: "Reversible MCTS as a Lens for Exposing Embedded Pre-Training Priors in Large Language Models." This directly appeals to the EMNLP zeitgeist, which highly values interpretability frameworks that shed light on why models behave the way they do during test-time interactions.1

### **Design 2: Empirical Validation of Dimensional Escape via Monitor Stress-Testing**

The existing premortem-v2.tex draft mathematically defines the Orthogonal Escape Index (OEI) and the Telemetry Divergence Score (TDS) utilizing the ![][image3] (H-Neuron) and ![][image4] (RepE honesty) channels.1 This outside-the-box experiment leverages the MCTS infrastructure to actively optimize for a high TDS. The search algorithm is deployed to intentionally discover KV-cache states where the RepE honesty probe reports high compliance (falsely indicating honesty) while the H-Neuron stress signal simultaneously indicates severe hallucination or over-compliance.24

This methodology utilizes the exact codebase currently running, entirely bypassing the need for a Goodhart-resistant "reasoning" reward. The resulting paper validates the hypothesis that linear monitors can be systematically bypassed via inference-time latent optimization. It proves that a static probe trained on baseline activations cannot adequately guard a continuous representation space against test-time scaling pressure. This positions the Reversible MCTS algorithm as a vital red-teaming tool for mechanistic anomaly detection, a subject that guarantees high relevance and engagement within the EMNLP alignment and interpretability tracks.1

### **Design 3: Latent Distillation for System-2 Trajectory Emulation**

Instead of using MCTS as an end-user tool to force a frozen model to output a correct answer at inference time, this experiment uses MCTS as an offline dataset generator. The algorithm is run on a specific subset of prompts where the correct answer is already known, using a strict ground-truth matching reward function. Because the reward is ground-truth exactness rather than a surrogate proxy, Goodhart’s Law is mathematically neutralized.

The output of this offline process is a dataset of continuous geometric trajectories—the exact sequence of FP32 KV-cache accumulator deltas required to navigate the model from its baseline state to a successful reasoning state.1 This continuous trajectory data is then distilled into a lightweight, parameter-efficient linear projector. The core experimental question is whether a static intervention, derived from dynamic MCTS search trajectories, can emulate continuous System-2 cognitive effort at a fraction of the computational cost.1 This bridges the gap between test-time scaling and representation engineering, offering a highly novel contribution that perfectly aligns with EMNLP's interest in efficient, scalable inference strategies.

## **Part 5: Connection to the Original Orthogonal-Escape Framing**

The original Hypothesis 1 of the premortem-v2.tex manuscript formulated the "Orthogonal Escape Hypothesis," positing that under corrective monitoring pressure, a deceptive model redistributes its internal representations into the null space of a linear monitor (![][image10]) to preserve malicious behavioral capabilities while appearing compliant.1 The empirical failure surfaced by Track F is structurally identical, but it occurs under *reward-optimization* pressure rather than *corrective-monitor* pressure.

In both instances, the underlying geometric pathology is identical: the high-dimensional continuous latent space possesses an overwhelming abundance of unconstrained vectors. When an optimization algorithm—whether a gradient-based input attack as described by Bailey et al. 2024 1 or an inference-time Reversible MCTS—applies localized pressure to the system, the architecture exploits these unconstrained dimensions. It satisfies the proxy metric mathematically without fulfilling the actual semantic objective.

**Is "orthogonal escape" the right framing for the broader phenomenon?**

Yes, but the terminology must be decoupled from the narrow, anthropomorphic domain of "deception." The broader framing should be rigorously defined as **Dimensional Escape**: the fundamental tendency of overparameterized latent spaces to resolve localized optimization pressure by routing representations through unconstrained orthogonal dimensions. Goal-directed deception (evading a monitor while maintaining behavior) is simply a specialized sub-case of this broader mathematical pathology. Reward hacking (minimizing entropy by collapsing to a pre-training prior) is the second sub-case.

**Will EMNLP Reviewers buy the unifying claim?** Reviewers in the mechanistic interpretability and alignment tracks are highly receptive to overarching geometric frameworks that synthesize seemingly disparate observations.1 If the paper explicitly claims to have built a general inference-time tool (Reversible MCTS) that exposes Dimensional Escape across *both* monitor evasion and reward hacking, reviewers will recognize it as a robust, unifying theory. It elevates the manuscript from a single point-observation of a failed experiment to a foundational structural analysis of LLM representational geometry. It will not be read as an overreach or a post-hoc rescue, provided the theoretical bridge is articulated clearly in the introduction.

## **Part 6: Wildcard Recommendation**

**The MCTS "Exhaust" as a Meta-Classifier Feature.**

Instead of viewing the terminal node of the MCTS tree as the ultimate output, the team should analyze the *search tree trajectory itself* as a topological feature of the model's epistemological uncertainty. Calculate the branching variance, the depth-of-rejection rate, and the magnitude of the FP32 accumulator deltas explored by the MCTS before it settles on its final configuration. A prompt that requires genuine, complex reasoning will yield a highly volatile MCTS search tree with massive variance across intermediate node values, whereas a prompt resting on a strong, hallucinated prior will yield a narrow, direct search tree. By training a simple logistic regression classifier solely on the metadata of the tree search (the "exhaust"), the team can predict whether the model's baseline answer is a hallucination or a grounded fact with exceptionally high accuracy, effectively turning the search dynamics themselves into a novel, zero-shot hallucination detector without requiring the search to successfully "beat" the benchmark.
