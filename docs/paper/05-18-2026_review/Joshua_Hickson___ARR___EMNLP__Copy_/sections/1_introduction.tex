Inference-time interventions on frozen language models---from one-shot activation steering to iterative test-time tree search---offer a frontier for both capability scaling and mechanistic analysis. As interventions move from heuristic perturbations to formal search procedures over a model's continuous latent state, a central question emerges: when controlled perturbations to a frozen model's internal representations are guided by a low-rank scoring projection (whether monitor, reward, or probe), do the search trajectories navigate the semantic manifold, or do they exploit the unconstrained dimensions of an overparameterized latent space?

This question has been posed in alignment-faking research---both as behavioral compliance under safety-training pressure~\citep{greenblatt2024alignment, hubinger2024sleeper} and as representational monitor evasion~\citep{gupta2025rlobfuscation}---and in gradient-trained input-space attacks against frozen models~\citep{bailey2024obfuscated}, the latter reporting a tentative negative result. These observations differ in intervention site, training regime, and objective but posit a single geometric pattern that we name \emph{Dimensional Escape}: localized optimization pressure on an overparameterized latent space resolves through the unconstrained directions of that space (§\ref{sec:dim_escape}).

We test the \emph{reward-driven} case, in which an inference-time tree search optimizes a surrogate reward defined on the frozen model's own internal signals. Distinguishing converged trajectories that produce semantically meaningful states from degenerate optima (reward maxima bypassing the answer-relevant subspace) requires search infrastructure with exact-rollback semantics across deep tree expansions. The Reversible KV-Cache MCTS framework introduced here provides this: Theorem~\ref{thm:reversibility} establishes zero-drift reversibility via an FP32 accumulator (validated to machine precision over 200 apply--revert cycles), and Proposition~\ref{prop:memory} establishes a memory bound independent of branching factor (measured at $3.04$--$3.06\times$ the base KV cache on Llama~3.2-1B/3B).

Across two experiments---a controlled negative-control on ARC-Easy (Experiment~1, §\ref{sec:exp1}) and Latent Cartography sweeps across four prompt classes (Experiment~2, §\ref{sec:exp2})---the reward-driven case manifests robustly. An entropy-minimization reward correlating with correctness at $|r| \approx 0.60$ collapses under MCTS at both 1B and 3B scales via distinct mechanisms (prior-amplification at 1B; greedy-collapse at 3B). Cartography further reveals a scale-conditional, class-conditional mechanism: MCQ classes flip from heavy-steering at 1B to minimal-steering at 3B (cross-scale $\Delta_{\bar\alpha} \approx 0.67$/$0.55$ on ARC-Easy/TruthfulQA; details in §\ref{sec:exp2}). We argue this reward-driven failure is structurally analogous to the monitor-driven sub-case hypothesized in the alignment-faking literature; Reversible KV-Cache MCTS is therefore most usefully positioned as a high-precision diagnostic probe of frozen-model representational geometry rather than a reasoning-improvement tool, with the monitor-driven sub-case left to Phase~B research.

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
    \item \textbf{Reversible Monte Carlo Tree Search in KV-cache latent space.} We introduce an inference-time algorithm that mutates a frozen Transformer's cached key/value tensors via in-place arithmetic ($\mathbf{K}_t \leftarrow \mathbf{K}_t + \alpha \cdot \mathbf{d}_K^{(\ell)}$) and inverts each mutation exactly through an FP32 accumulator. We prove zero-drift reversibility (Theorem~\ref{thm:reversibility}) and validate it empirically on Llama~3.2-1B/3B-Instruct (drift $= 0$ to machine precision over 200 apply--revert cycles). The working-set memory complexity is $O(M_{\text{KV}} + d \cdot K_{\text{acc}})$ (Proposition~\ref{prop:memory}) rather than the $O(b^d \cdot M_{\text{KV}})$ of standard parallel-cache MCTS---reducing the memory floor from exponential in $b^d$ to additive in $d$. The accumulator overhead is independent of branching factor in the bound, measured at $3.04$--$3.06\times M_{\text{KV}}$ above the baseline cache across the tested depth and prefill configurations on Llama~3.2-1B/3B. We position this algorithm as reusable interpretability infrastructure: a memory-bounded, exact-rollback probe for studying continuous-latent search behavior on any frozen Transformer, applicable to monitor-driven and reward-driven scoring projections beyond the entropy reward we test in §\ref{sec:exp1}. The algorithm is gradient-free and operates on a frozen model at inference time, distinguishing it from gradient-based input-space attacks~\citep{zou2023gcg, bailey2024obfuscated} and training-time monitor-evasion methods~\citep{gupta2025rlobfuscation}.

    \item \textbf{Empirical demonstration of reward-driven Dimensional Escape---an instance of Goodhart's Law in high-dimensional latent-space search.} We establish a geometric pathology of MCTS in overparameterized latent spaces: the entropy-normalized surrogate reward we test resolves localized optimization pressure by routing representations through unconstrained dimensions, succumbing to prior-amplification or greedy-collapse rather than satisfying the semantic objective; we conjecture this extends to surrogate rewards lacking exogenous grounding more generally (§\ref{sec:dim_escape}). Two complementary experiments support the claim. \emph{Experiment 1} (negative control, §\ref{sec:exp1}): on $n = 200$ paired ARC-Easy items at 1B and 3B, an entropy-minimization reward---statically correlated with correctness at $|r| \approx 0.60$---collapses under MCTS to within $0.5$pp of a uniform-random reward (paired McNemar $p = 1.0$ at both scales), via prior-amplification at 1B (predicting ``A'' on 185 of 200 items) and greedy-collapse at 3B (minimal-magnitude steering on 51\% of items). \emph{Experiment 2} (Latent Cartography, §\ref{sec:exp2}): depth-$3$ entropy-MCTS path sampling across four prompt classes (factual recall, ARC-Easy, TruthfulQA, HellaSwag continuation) demonstrates that the failure manifests across domains and that the terminal-token distribution varies systematically with the prompt's semantic class. We further argue the reward-driven pathology surfaced here is structurally analogous to the monitor-driven case hypothesized in the alignment-faking literature and partially characterized by \citet{bailey2024obfuscated} for gradient-trained input attacks: we conjecture both as sub-cases of the same overparameterized-latent-space geometry (§\ref{sec:dim_escape}). The unified framing positions Reversible MCTS as a high-precision diagnostic probe of frozen-model representational geometry rather than a reasoning-improvement tool.
\end{enumerate}
