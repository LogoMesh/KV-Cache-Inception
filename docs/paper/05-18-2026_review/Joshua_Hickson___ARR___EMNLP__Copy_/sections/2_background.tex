Strategic misrepresentation of alignment is a longstanding concern in AI safety~\citep{hubinger2024sleeper, greenblatt2024alignment}. Recent work has demonstrated frontier-scale strategic compliance~\citep{greenblatt2024alignment} and the persistence of deliberately inserted backdoors through standard safety training~\citep{hubinger2024sleeper}, both with strong scale-dependence. These findings suggest that behavioral evaluations alone are insufficient for reliable alignment detection, motivating the inference-time latent-space analysis on frozen models that this paper develops.

Representation Engineering (RepE)~\citep{zou2023repe} established that high-level cognitive states are linearly decodable from a model's residual stream, enabling activation steering~\citep{turner2023steering} and one-shot cache steering~\citep{belitsky2025kvcache}---both operating in a corrective paradigm with fixed precomputed vectors. Our MCTS instead iteratively explores the KV-cache intervention space across multiple rollouts, reversing each intervention exactly via FP32 accumulators and guided by an inference-time surrogate reward (Eq.~\ref{eq:goodhart}) rather than a fixed behavioral objective. \citet{gupta2025rlobfuscation} showed that models can learn to evade latent-space monitors under RL finetuning, with monitor-aggregation-class generalization across unseen probes---providing analogous motivation for Hypothesis~\ref{hyp:monitor_driven}, although their training-time regime differs from our frozen-weight inference-time setting.

Bailey et al.~\citep{bailey2024obfuscated} systematically study whether gradient-trained adversarial perturbations to model inputs can achieve activation states that are simultaneously behavior-preserving and orthogonal to baseline activations, and report a tentative negative result for that regime: near-orthogonality and behavior preservation cannot be jointly achieved, since pushing cosine similarity to the level of unrelated inputs requires a KL divergence that produces incoherent output. Our work is complementary along three axes: (i)~the intervention is applied to cached key/value tensors at inference time rather than to input embeddings during gradient training; (ii)~the search optimizes a surrogate reward on the next-token distribution (Eq.~\ref{eq:goodhart}) rather than minimizing similarity to baseline; (iii)~we characterize how a surrogate-reward MCTS resolves the reward through latent-space trajectories (Experiments~\ref{sec:exp1}--\ref{sec:exp2}), leaving open whether the inference-time, frozen-weight, surrogate-reward setting exhibits the same geometric constraint.
