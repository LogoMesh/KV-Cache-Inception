\section{Ethics Statement}
Our work addresses a critical bottleneck in AI safety research. It systematically designs effective red-teaming systems with minimal human supervision by drawing inspiration from existing literature and iteratively reflecting and refining based on the archive of discovered systems. This work could potentially serve as a scalable and accessible pipeline for comprehensive safety testing, allowing safety researchers to identify risks before model deployment. Additionally, this work highlights the practical application of evolutionary algorithms to real-world scientific challenges.

Like other red-teaming methods, our approach could potentially be misused by malicious actors to elicit illicit and harmful content from language models. The automated system optimization process might lower the barrier to developing sophisticated jailbreaking systems.

Overall, we believe that as a scalable approach to accelerate the AI safety research and a working example of applying evolutionary algorithm to advance science, the contribution of our work to the broader community substantially outweighs its risks.
