Every individual listed as a co-author on the "KV-Cache Inception" manuscript must possess an active, fully updated OpenReview profile prior to the abstract submission deadline on May 4\.14 The system relies on the metadata within these profiles to execute the automated double-blind conflict-of-interest (COI) matching algorithms, pairing the paper with appropriate Area Chairs and reviewers.

The administrative setup of these profiles harbors a significant trap for newly formed research teams or external collaborators. If any member of the team attempts to create a new OpenReview profile utilizing a generic, public email domain (e.g., @gmail.com, @yahoo.com, @163.com), the system will flag the account and place it into a manual moderation queue.14 This moderation process is designed to combat spam and fake profiles, and it can take up to **two weeks** to complete.14 If a co-author's profile is locked in moderation when the deadline arrives, the submission cannot be finalized. To bypass this catastrophic delay, all team members must be explicitly instructed to register using verified, institutional email addresses (such as .edu or recognized corporate domains), which triggers instantaneous, automated profile activation.37

Furthermore, all authors must meticulously update their "Education & Career History" and "Advisors & Other Relations" sections within their profiles.14 They are encouraged to import their publication history, ideally via DBLP integration, to facilitate accurate reviewer matching.14 Failure to comprehensively declare institutional conflicts, recent co-authorship networks, or advisor relationships fundamentally undermines the integrity of the double-blind review process. NeurIPS deploys sophisticated automated tools to detect undeclared conflicts, and violations are treated as severe breaches of academic integrity, potentially triggering immediate removal from the reviewing system, rejection of the paper, and sanctions regarding future conference participation.14

Finally, the team must recognize the principle of authorship lock. Once the full paper registration deadline passes on May 6, the author list is frozen. The conference policies explicitly dictate that no author additions or removals will be accepted after this point.39 Therefore, the final author block must be definitively negotiated, agreed upon, and accurately reflected in the OpenReview system well in advance of the final submission cascade.

The "KV-Cache Inception" manuscript is a formidable piece of research that tackles one of the most pressing and intractable vulnerabilities in contemporary artificial intelligence: the capacity for advanced models to strategically fake alignment. The conference's procedural architecture has evolved into a highly complex, interconnected web of epistemological track boundaries, rigorous data documentation standards, and uncompromising ethical oversight. To successfully navigate this environment and maximize the probability of high-impact reception, the research team must execute a coordinated, multi-layered submission strategy:

This ensures the manuscript is judged by specialists in AI safety and dataset governance, rather than generalized algorithmic theorists.
